Petroleum Sector Data Analysis
Visualization & Trend Study (India, 2022–2024)

Overview

This presentation summarizes the results of a comprehensive analysis of India’s petroleum sector using five cleaned datasets. The study covers crude oil production, refinery output, product output, domestic consumption, and international trade.

  • Data period: 2022–2024
  • Goal: Understand trends, relationships, and prepare data for forecasting
Slide 1 of 15

Data Preparation

All datasets were cleaned in R. Column names were standardized (camelCase), and missing values replaced using interpolation or average substitution. A uniform date column was created for time-series plotting.

The five datasets include:

  • Crude Oil Production
  • Refinery Output
  • Product Output
  • Product Consumption
  • Product Trade
Slide 2 of 15

Crude Oil Production — Trend

India’s crude production remains largely stable between 8–9 million tonnes per month, showing minimal growth. A brief dip around early 2023 may reflect maintenance shutdowns or reduced field output.

Slide 3 of 15

Crude Production by Company

ONGC and OIL dominate production, accounting for most of India’s domestic output. The contribution of smaller players remains relatively minor.

Slide 4 of 15

Refinery Production

Refinery output shows an upward trend, confirming India’s position as a global refining hub. Growth is stable, reflecting both domestic and export-oriented refining capacity.

Slide 5 of 15

Refinery Output — Company Comparison

Company-level analysis shows consistent operation among top refineries (IOCL, ONGC, RIL). IOCL remains the largest refiner, maintaining dominance across months.

Slide 6 of 15

Product Output — Major Fuels

Among refined products, Diesel (HSD) and Petrol (MS) show the highest production. LPG, Naphtha, and ATF maintain smaller but stable outputs, following refinery throughput patterns.

Slide 7 of 15

Product Consumption — Domestic Trends

Diesel and Petrol lead domestic consumption, representing transport and industrial demand. LPG shows slight seasonality, with peaks during colder months.

Slide 8 of 15

Output vs Consumption

Consumption trends largely mirror production, implying efficient refinery-to-market flow. Minor mismatches indicate inventory adjustments or export allocations.

Slide 9 of 15

Trade Overview

India imports most of its crude oil but exports refined products. This trade pattern adds significant value through domestic refining and supports foreign exchange earnings.

Slide 10 of 15

Import vs Export Composition

Crude oil and LPG dominate imports, while Diesel and Petrol dominate exports. This highlights India’s dependence on crude imports and its refining advantage for export markets.

Slide 11 of 15

Trade Balance Trend

Total import values surpass export values due to high global crude prices, though exports partially offset the import bill. Value per tonne fluctuates with global oil prices.

Slide 12 of 15

Key Findings

  • Domestic crude output stagnant → high import dependency.
  • Refinery output rising → India’s strong refining capability.
  • Diesel & Petrol dominate both production and consumption.
  • Trade shows value addition through refined exports.

Next Steps — Forecasting

The cleaned datasets enable time-series forecasting for future months:

  • Crude production — ARIMA
  • Consumption — ARIMA or Prophet
  • Trade values — Regression / ETS

Conclusion

This analysis establishes a solid base for forecasting India’s petroleum trends. Visualizations highlight stable crude output, growing refinery capacity, and a strong export footprint for refined products. Part 2 will focus on forecasting and predictive modeling.
